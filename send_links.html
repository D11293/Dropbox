Send links to your work

Quickly share a copy of your work with others, even if they don't have a Dropbox account.

Shared links are perfect for sending work to external partners or even other people on your team. They're like email attachments, but simpler and without the file-size limit.

Key points:

  • Recipients don't need a Dropbox account and they'll get an instant preview of the linked file or folder through their browser.
  • You can even give your links passwords and expiration dates to control who's able to access them.
  • Links are dynamic, which means that whenever they're accessed online they link to the most current version of your file or folder. No need to keep re-sending links as you update your work!
  • They can still download and edit a copy of the files you share, but they never access or edit your original work.

Sending links from your computer

To send a link from your computer, just follow these steps:

  1. Open your Dropbox folder.
  2. Right-click (or control-click on a Mac) on the file to bring up a menu.
  3. Choose Share Dropbox link. The link will be copied and you can immediately paste it anywhere.

Sending links using the Dropbox website

To share a link to a folder:

  1. Sign in into the Dropbox website (if you haven't already).
  2. Hover over the folder you would like to share and click on the "Share" button that appears on the right.
  3. Select the "Send link..."" option.
  4. You can copy the URL and paste it into an email, instant message, etc., or just add the recipient's email and we'll send the link for you.
  5. If you want, select the Set password / expiration button to add a password, set an expiration date, or restrict access to just members of your Business account.

To share a link to a file:

  1. Sign in into the Dropbox website (if you haven't already).
  2. Hover over the file you would like to share and click on the "Share" button that appears on the right.
  3. You can copy the URL and paste it into an email, instant message, etc., or just add the recipient's email and we'll send the link for you.
  4. If you want, select the Set password / expiration button to add a password, set an expiration date, or restrict access to just members of your Business account.

Managing your links

You can view all the links you've created or accessed from the Links tab on the left-hand sidebar of the Dropbox website.

  • "To remove a link, just click the "x" to the right of its name.
  • If you're sharing sensitive material, you can control who can see your link by adding a password, setting an expiration date, or making the link accessible to team members only.

To change access settings on a shared link:

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website (if you haven't already).
  2. Hover over the folder you would like to share and click on the "Share" button that appears on the right.
  3. Click Share link from the drop-down menu.
  4. Click the Set visibility/expiration button.
