Organize your folders

Easily manage your files by creating a simple folder structure.

Dropbox isn't like traditional file solutions - so you don't have to structure your folders the traditional way, either. There's no "right way" to set up your Dropbox, but here are some best practices to keep you and your team organized.

Understanding folder types

When stuff is stored in your Dropbox, you can access your work from anywhere and easily share it with others.

These are the types of folders you'll see inside your Dropbox:

  • Unshared: By default, all folders are unshared. Only you can see these folders.
  • Shared: A shared folder is a community space where members can collaborate together on the same files.
  • Team: A special type of shared folder that only Business admins can create, these automatically appear in your Dropbox when you join a team.

Organizing your folders

In general, it's easiest to organize files based on who needs access. Here are two folder structures based on common workflows:

Organize by team

Organize your folders by department, so team members only see the documents related to their work.

Organize by project or client

Creating a shared folder for each project or client lets team members coordinate with specific members without worrying about giving the wrong person access.
