Share your work

Collaborate with shared folders

Use a shared folder to create a central space for your team to collaborate on files.

All folders in Dropbox are private by default, but you can make any normal folder a shared folder. When you share a folder, its contents will only be available to Dropbox users you specifically invite. Once a user accepts your invitation, the shared folder appears in their Dropbox, just like it does in yours.

Key points:

  • Shared folders are the best way to collaborate using Dropbox.
  • Members of a shared folder can edit and access the same files.
  • You can assign some members full edit rights and others view-only access to prevent them from adding, editing, or removing files.
  • You can also set access controls to determine if users outside your Dropbox for Business team are allowed to join your shared folder.

Sharing a folder

A shared folder icon has two little people on it (see above).

To create a new shared folder:

  1. Sign in if you haven't already.
  2. Click on the rainbow folder icon at the top center of your screen.
  3. Select "I'd like to create and share a new folder".
  4. Name your new shared folder.
  5. Enter the email addresses of the people you want to invite, and add a personal message if you'd like.
  6. Set permissions and folder settings (see below), and then click Share folder.

To share a folder from your computer:

  1. Open your Dropbox folder.
  2. Right-click (or control-click on a Mac) on the folder you want to share to bring up a menu.
  3. Select Share This Folder... This will open the Sharing page on the Dropbox website.
  4. Enter the email addresses of the people you want to invite, and add a personal message if you'd like.
  5. Set permissions and folder settings (see below), and then click Share folder.

To share an existing folder from the website:

  1. Sign in if you haven't already.
  2. Hover over the folder you would like to share and click on the "Share" button that appears on the right hand side.
  3. Select the "Invite people to collaborate..." option.
  4. Enter the email addresses of the people you want to invite, and add a personal message if you'd like.
  5. Set permissions and folder settings (see below), and then click Share folder.

Setting access permissions

There are three roles in a shared folder:

  • Owner can change view/edit permissions, invite additional people, and transfer ownership to someone else. This is the default role for whoever initially shares a folder.
  • Editor can add/edit/delete files in the folder just like an owner. If the owner allows it, editors can also invite people to the folder, remove other members from the folder, and set the roles of other members. Editor is the default setting for the people you invite.
  • Viewer is the most limited role and can't add/edit/delete files in the folder. A viewer will be able to tell that they have view-only permissions because of the lock icon that appears on the shared folder:

To invite viewers to a shared folder:

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website.
  2. Select the folder you want to share by right-clicking (or control-clicking on a Mac).
  3. Click Shared folder options.
  4. Click can edit next to the member's name and choose can view from the drop-down menu.
  5. Click can view from the pull-down menu at the bottom.

To change editors to viewers:

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website.
  2. Select the folder you want to share by right-clicking (or control-clicking on a Mac)..
  3. Click Invite to folder...(or Shared folder options if the folder is already shared).
  4. Type in the email address of the new member you'd like to add.
  5. Click can view from the pull-down menu at the bottom.

Changing sharing settings as the folder owner

If you're the owner of the shared folder, you can decide if other members can invite users to your folder, and if they can change other users' access permissions.

In addition, Business users can decide to make shared folders "team only" - a helpful tool for folders containing sensitive material. When enabled, this permission setting prevents external users from being invited to join a folder.

To adjust a shared folder's sharing settings:

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website (if you haven't already).
  2. Right-click (or control click on a Mac) on the shared folder you want to change. A new window will appear.
  3. Click the Change sharing settings link at the top of the window.
  4. Select who can be invited and who can change permissions.

Note: Admins have additional sharing controls via the Admin Console. Please check with them or visit our Help Center to learn more.
