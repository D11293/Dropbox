Security

Encourage two-step verification

Encourage your team members to add an extra layer of protection to their accounts by using two-step verification.

Two-step verification is an optional but highly recommended security feature. In addition to their username and password, two-step verification requires users to sign into Dropbox with a six-digit security code available only from their mobile phone.

You can require that two-step verification stays on once your team members enable it. If a team member loses their phone and is unable to access the required code to sign in, you can turn off the security feature in the Admin Console.

Reminding your team to turn on two-step verification

You cannot enable two-step verification on behalf of your team members because it requires access to their mobile phones. You can check whether or not your team members have activated two-step verification, and also send them reminders to set up this security feature.

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website using your Business admin account.
  2. Click on Admin Console in the left-hand sidebar.
  3. Click on Authentication in the sidebar.
  4. In the "Two-step verification" section, click Send a reminder to team members who still have it disabled.

Ensuring two-step verification stays on

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website using your Business admin account.
  2. Click on Admin Console in the left-hand sidebar.
  3. Click on Authentication in the sidebar.
  4. Under "Two-Step Verification," select the box next to Require two-step verification to stay on.

Disabling two-step verification

If a team member loses their mobile device and cannot receive their two-step verification code, you can disable this security feature for them so they can sign in to their account.

  1. Sign in to the Dropbox website using your Business admin account.
  2. Click on Admin Console in the left-hand sidebar.
  3. From the Members page, click on the gear icon next to the member's name.
  4. Select Disable two-step verification.
